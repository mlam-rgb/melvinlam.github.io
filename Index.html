Melvin Lam

Musician and Berklee student interested in music, creativity, and building sustainable paths for artists.

Bio

I’m a flutist and creative musician based in Cambridge. Alongside performing and studying music business, I’m interested in how artists grow, find direction, and navigate the space between creativity and sustainability.

What I Care About Right Now

Right now I’m focused on learning new tools and concepts that help me better understand how to create real solutions for artists who are growing musically but feel stuck when it comes to direction, visibility, or structure. I’m curious about why some artists with real talent find momentum while others struggle to move forward, even when the potential is clearly there. I’m especially interested in frameworks and ways of thinking that align with how I already approach problems and allow me to go deeper instead of reacting on instinct. This matters to me because I want to develop ideas that support artists in that unclear middle stage between growth and sustainability.
